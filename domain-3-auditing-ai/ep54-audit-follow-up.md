# AI Audit Follow-up: Tracking, Automation, and Verification

Tracking issue resolution in artificial intelligence is a continuous, real-time process compared to traditional audits — and software integration now allows for automated validation of those fixes. This episode of the **ISACA Advanced in AI Audit (AAIA)** exam prep series explains how this shift fundamentally changes the way you manage your daily workflow and team resources. Instead of scheduling block time for manual reviews months after an audit, you can design automated dashboards that alert you the moment a compliance gap is closed, freeing you to focus on higher-level strategic risk assessment rather than chasing down routine patch updates.

## Breaking Away From the Conventional Follow-up

In a standard technology audit, you might finish your report, leave the department to do its work, and come back three months later to see if they fixed the problems. Artificial intelligence development moves entirely too fast for that delayed approach. Because AI models are constantly being updated, trained, and deployed, the auditor stays right there alongside the developers, observing the project as it actually goes live.

You will frequently encounter the term **remediation**, which simply means the act of fixing a discovered weakness or correcting a vulnerability. Imagine a building inspector: traditionally they inspect the foundation, leave, and come back weeks later to inspect the framing. In an AI audit, the inspector is standing on the construction site every single day. When a worker hammers in a missing nail, the inspector sees it happen and immediately checks it off the list. Because you are watching the fixes happen in real time, you can close out open audit findings much faster.

## Automated Follow-up and System Integration

As corporate software systems become more deeply connected, we can remove the human bottleneck from the follow-up process. Instead of an auditor manually checking if a fix was applied, the systems themselves talk to each other to confirm the job is done.

The formal term you need to know here is **validation of closed action items** — the official process of proving that a required fix was not just attempted, but actually works as intended. Think about a modern smart home thermostat: you do not need to walk to the basement to check if the furnace turned on, because the thermostat automatically detects the temperature change and updates the display on your phone. Similarly, in a highly integrated technical ecosystem, the moment an engineering team deploys a security patch, the system automatically sends a verified signal to the audit tracking software. The audit item closes itself based on system-generated proof, completely eliminating the need for manual verification.

## Why This Matters for the Modern Auditor

This continuous, real-time presence drastically speeds up the closure of open issues and changes how auditors allocate their time. By linking systems together across the organization, the technology itself confirms when an action item is successfully resolved, so auditors are no longer tied up in repetitive manual reviews. That shift lets the audit function move from chasing routine patch updates toward higher-level strategic risk assessment — exactly where an auditor's judgment adds the most value.
